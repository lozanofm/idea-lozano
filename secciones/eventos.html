THE ROGUE CHALLENGES

Since 2015, Rogue Record Breakers has provided a showcase for unprecedented feats of strength, including historical world records that span the Iron Game. We put the RRB into an online format in 2019, and we’re now pleased to announce the expansion of that line-up. In the Rogue Challenge, anyone, anywhere, is welcome to sign-up and compete for a spot on the leaderboard or a page in the record books. When your challenge begins, be sure to use #ryourogue and @roguefitness to share your experience. You can also keep up with the leaderboard and current record holders at roguefitness.com/challenges. *If you don’t have an existing BTWB account, one will be automatically created for you once you pay your registration fee.

THE OVERALL CHALLENGE WINNERS

This Challenge will reward hard work and consistency across all of the Rogue challenges. Athletes are automatically awarded points based on their placement for each challenge. Weight and age divisions are combined so athlete scores are compared with one another to determine the top scores. The top three male and female competitors with the most points across all Rogue Challenges at the end of the year win.

COMPETITION RULES

In order to be eligible for a prize, athletes must have submitted videos of their performances following the rules of each individual challenge completed during the corresponding challenge time frame. If a competitor has not submitted a video during the challenge window, they will remain on the individual event leaderboard but not count toward the overall leaderboard Athletes can not submit a video following the close of the challenge window Videos will not be requested at a later date or during the final overall leaderboard judging/finalization process Overall Challenge is open to RX'd athletes only. Scores on the overall leaderboard are unofficial until winners are announced following the completion of the 2022 Challenge season 1st place receives 100 points, 2nd place receives 99 points - down to 98th place and lower receive 2 points. If athletes register but do not submit a score, they will receive 0 points
